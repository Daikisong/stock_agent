# Round-29 Risk Boundary Review

Round 29 separates Green-possible structures from Watch-first cycle/risk areas.

## Green-Possible With Strict Gates
- DEFENSE_GOVERNMENT_BACKLOG: Defense can be Green, but backlog, delivery, margin, and capital allocation must be source-backed.
- SHIPBUILDING_OFFSHORE_BACKLOG: Shipbuilding needs price and margin recognition; backlog quantity alone is not Green evidence.
- EXPORT_RECURRING_CONSUMER: Export consumer Green does not require contract quality; repeat demand and channel evidence matter.
- RAIL_INFRASTRUCTURE: Rail is contract-backlog style, but project margin, delivery, and financing deserve stronger gates.
- SECURITY_IDENTITY_DEEPFAKE: Security can be Green with recurring revenue, but operational trust is a hard gate.
- INSURANCE_UNDERWRITING: Insurance is PBR-ROE-return rerating, not classic EPS explosion; underwriting and capital safety are gates.
- VALUE_UP_SHAREHOLDER: Value-up needs actual return execution and ROE/NAV improvement; low PBR or index label is not enough.

## Watch-First / 4C-Sensitive
- CHEMICAL_SPREAD: spread_reversal, capacity_glut, inventory_build, demand_slowdown
- DIGITAL_ASSET_TOKENIZATION: regulatory_delay, security_incident, liquidity_collapse, no_revenue_model
- RETAIL_ECOMMERCE_LOGISTICS: data_security_breach, supplier_regulation_hit, logistics_cost_spike, inventory_build
- BATTERY_MATERIALS_ESS: ev_demand_slowdown, mineral_price_collapse, capex_overbuild, policy_reversal
- SECURITIES_BROKERAGE: pf_loss, proprietary_loss, turnover_collapse, ipo_cycle_freeze

## Rule
- Stage 3-Green still requires cross-evidence and price-path validation.
- Chemicals, digital assets, ecommerce, battery/ESS, and securities are Watch-first because cycle, regulation, security, or FCF risk is high.
- Defense, shipbuilding, export consumer, rail, security, insurance, and value-up are Green-possible only with source-backed economics.
- Case records are calibration examples, not production labels.
