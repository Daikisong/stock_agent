## 7. Interpretation

C25 works like a three-valve pipe. The first valve is device/product acceptance, the second is reimbursement/export/procedure-volume confirmation, and the third is revenue/margin conversion. A label can splash water into the pipe, but without the second and third valves opening, the stock path often becomes a short local spike followed by high MAE. This loop therefore does not propose a global healthcare relaxation. It proposes a C25-specific bridge: **device/export/reimbursement labels require procedure-volume or revenue-margin proof before Yellow or Green**.

Positive cases in this loop share lower MAE and a cleaner export/procedure/revenue bridge: `206640`, `263690`, `042520`, `225220`. Counterexamples share label strength without enough conversion evidence: `290650`, `950130`, `049180`, `258830`. The hard-4C subtype is clearest in post-COVID diagnostic export revenue-break style paths.

## 8. Current Calibrated Profile Stress Test

- `stage2_required_bridge` remains correct, but C25 needs a subtype bridge: procedure-volume / export acceptance / reimbursement / revenue-margin conversion.
- `local_4b_watch_guard` should be strict when device-label spikes show weak 90D MFE and deepening 90D/180D MAE.
- `hard_4c_confirmation` should require non-price break evidence such as revenue-margin collapse, customer/procedure-volume fade, reimbursement delay, or demand reset.
- `price_only_blowoff_blocks_positive_stage` remains valid; this loop adds C25-specific labels that should be blocked until real bridge confirmation.
