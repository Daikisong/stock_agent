## 13. Current Calibrated Profile Stress Test
Residual errors found: generic EV-demand slowdown can be too blunt when a battery input-material or component route shows a recovery band with contained 90D MAE; the opposite error also appears when orderbook/capacity memory delays hard 4C after confirmed demand/utilization/margin break. C14 therefore needs a two-door filter: recovery-band exception gate before hard 4C, and confirmed demand-pull/margin-break gate before routing to Stage4C.

## 14. Stage2 / Yellow / Green Comparison

Green lateness ratio is recorded as `not_applicable:no_confirmed_Stage3_Green_trigger` in JSONL. For the three positive rows, Stage2-Actionable captured a recovery band while the proposed shadow profile still refuses Green without verified margin/revision bridge.

## 15. 4B Local vs Full-window Timing Audit
The C14 local 4B rows show that price-only local peaks are not enough for full 4B, but they are sufficient for a local watch overlay when valuation blowoff, positioning overheat, or margin/backlog slowdown is present. The SK Innovation row is local 4B rather than hard 4C because integrated refining/chemical cash flow can partially offset battery weakness. The POSCO Future M, Sangsin EDP, and Solus rows are routed harder because the MFE/MAE path aligns with demand-reset and utilization-break evidence.

## 16. 4C Protection Audit
Hard 4C protection is useful only when the thesis break is non-price and specific. Generic EV slowdown language alone is too broad. In this loop, confirmed or candidate 4C applies to cathode material blowoff fade, battery-can component demand reset, and copper-foil customer-pull/utilization break. Aluminum/lightweighting and separator/fluoropolymer recovery-band cases are marked as false-break exceptions.

## 17. Sector-Specific Rule Candidate
