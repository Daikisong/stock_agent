## 5. Case-level interpretation

### 5.1 Positive controls

**232140 / 와이씨**  
The early March 2024 trigger is deliberately before the formal HBM contract/shipment news. The case says C09 should not wait for the last legal event when a memory-wafer tester identity, customer/mix bridge, and sector setup already exist. If the model waits until the July contract or November first shipment, the price path turns into a late-entry counterexample. The right interpretation is early Stage2-Actionable with explicit later 4B protection.

**140860 / 파크시스템스**  
This is a non-HBM-label positive. The company is an AFM/nano-metrology equipment platform where semiconductor process complexity and advanced packaging create customer-specific demand. The residual is that C09 should not be too narrow: advanced equipment rerating can be metrology-driven even when the ticker is not an obvious HBM back-end name.

**322310 / 오로스테크놀로지, November 2024 reset**  
The same symbol failed as a March blowoff, but the November trigger occurs after valuation reset and with a new tool/progress signal. The rule should not permanently blacklist a ticker after one C09 false positive. It should reopen when the price base has reset and the evidence family changes from theme to product/tool progress.

### 5.2 Counterexamples

**322310 / March 2024 late report-title blowoff**  
The March report had a real HBM/overlay metrology story, but it arrived after the price was already stretched. Without incremental order, qualification, or revision evidence, the row behaves like a late Stage3-Yellow false positive and should have been local 4B watch.

**348210 / 넥스틴**  
The March 2024 report contained real pattern-defect inspection, HBM, China, and backlog language. The later customer cancellation exposed the missing durability check. The model needs a customer-concentration and contract-cancel guard, because “scarce inspection equipment” does not automatically mean durable rerating.

**089890 / 코세스**  
The copper-pin attach / advanced-packaging optionality produced a tradable MFE but then a deep 180D MAE. This is the shape C09 most easily mishandles: the spark is real, but without order lock or revenue conversion the move should be handled as Stage2/Watch plus local 4B, not Green.

**098460 / 고영**  
ZenStar and AI semiconductor inspection created a plausible equipment story, but product launch alone did not convert to order/revenue evidence by the entry. C09 should treat product launch as necessary but insufficient.

## 7. Shadow rule candidate

```text
C09_CONVERSION_BRIDGE_BEFORE_BLOWOFF_CREDIT
```

Candidate behavior:

1. Add C09 credit when at least two hard bridge fields exist:
   - equipment identity in HBM, advanced packaging, inspection, test, metrology, or advanced-node process control
   - named or inferable customer/memory/foundry fit
   - order, qualification, shipment, or product-tool progress
   - revenue/margin or mix-improvement visibility

2. Penalize or block Stage2-Actionable / Stage3-Yellow when the evidence is only:
   - product launch without order/revenue conversion
   - analyst/report title after price extension
   - generic HBM/AI semiconductor label
   - one-day relative strength
   - customer concentration without cancellation/durability check

3. Keep 4B conservative but earlier:
   - price-only fast MFE can create local 4B watch
   - full 4B requires non-price deterioration or thesis break
   - after a prior failed blowoff, reset-base plus new evidence can reopen Stage2

Confidence: **medium-low** because this is a single-loop candidate. Apply only through shadow calibration and later aggregate promotion logic, not directly to production.
