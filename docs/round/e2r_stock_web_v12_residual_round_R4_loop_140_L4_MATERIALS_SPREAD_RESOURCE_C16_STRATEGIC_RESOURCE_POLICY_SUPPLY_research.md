## 4. Case Notes

### 4.1 Direct positive: Korea Zinc TC benchmark and Q2 conversion

The cleanest C16 positive is `010130`. The April 2024 Teck/Korea Zinc TC benchmark cut is a direct supply-chain economics event: the Reuters article says Teck agreed to pay Korea Zinc USD 165/t to turn zinc concentrate into metal, down 40% from the previous year, and explains that TCs fall when mine output declines and smelters compete for concentrate. This is not just a policy word; it is a named counterparty + benchmark price + physical smelter economics event.

The second positive row is the August 2024 Q2 earnings bridge. The Q2 summary shows sales, OP and OPM conversion driven by higher metals and FX. That matters because C16 policy/supply events should not receive Yellow unless they begin to look like margin conversion rather than slogan exposure.

### 4.2 Proxy trap: KZ Precision and control-premium event mechanics

`036560` is useful as a C16 proxy counterexample. It has pump/valve/foundry process-equipment exposure, but broad zinc concentrate tightness does not automatically convert into order backlog, customer quality, or margin bridge for the proxy. The later upside was dominated by the Korea Zinc / Young Poong Precision control battle. That is why the 2024-09-26 row is a local 4B guardrail, not a fresh C16 Green.

### 4.3 Actual smelter, bad risk gate: Young Poong

`000670` is not merely a proxy: Young Poong’s own site says Sukpo smelter has annual zinc-ingot capacity of 400,000 tons and 99.995% purity. But the later Seokpo suspension confirms that real resource capacity can be impaired by legal/environmental risk. The November 2024 4C evidence is narratively strong, but the 180D raw price window is blocked from weight calibration due suspected corporate-action / share-price discontinuity in the local stock-web raw-unadjusted path.
