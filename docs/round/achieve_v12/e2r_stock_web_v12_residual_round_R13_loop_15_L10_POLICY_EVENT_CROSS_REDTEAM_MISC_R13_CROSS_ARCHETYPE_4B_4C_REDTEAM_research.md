The R13 split is not “MAE bad, reject.” Some high-MAE rows are protective successes or true thesis-break rows. The residual is whether the drawdown is backed by real thesis damage or merely by a temporary shock. In trader terms, MAE is not the illness; it is the fever. The diagnosis comes from the bridge: conversion evidence, legal break, customer/order proof, or explicit thesis failure.
