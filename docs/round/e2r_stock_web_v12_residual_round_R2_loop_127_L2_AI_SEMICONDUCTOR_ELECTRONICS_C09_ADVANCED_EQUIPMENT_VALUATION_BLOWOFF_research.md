## 4. Interpretation

### 4.1 What current profile still gets wrong

The residual C09 problem is **not** merely “valuation blowoff.” The harder residual is scope compression. A real HBM process component, a chiller/scrubber subsystem, an EUV pellicle prototype, EUV PR/CMP slurry, and special gas are all close enough to the advanced semiconductor stack to attract the same market attention. But they are not equally strong E2R signals.

- `GST` is the cleanest positive-control row: subsystem equipment, major-customer base, service/maintenance route, and 2024 revenue/OP bridge are visible, and the stock-web path shows 180D MFE of 96.88% with only -0.96% MAE.
- `Mico` and `Dongjin Semichem` are boundary positives: the HBM/advanced-process connection is real, but fast reprice and severe post-peak drawdown require local 4B overlay before durable Yellow.
- `FST`, `Wonik Materials`, and `Unisem` demonstrate over-promotion risk: product capability, special-gas recovery, or chiller sales improvement can produce early MFE, but without named order/repeat revenue/margin survival they fall into deep 90D/180D drawdowns.

### 4.2 Shadow rule candidate

```text
C09_PROCESS_COMPONENT_CREDIT_REQUIRES_ORDER_OR_REVENUE_CONVERSION_WITH_MATERIAL_PROXY_BLOCK_AND_POST_REPRICE_4B_CAP
```

Rule text:

```text
For C09, advanced equipment identity earns only Stage2-watch unless process role plus named customer/order/qualification or revenue-margin conversion is present. Process components can receive Stage2-Actionable boundary credit, but materials/consumables require scope discount, and fast 30D/90D MFE followed by deep 180D MAE routes to local/full-window 4B rather than durable Yellow.
```
