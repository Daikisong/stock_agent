## 1. Current Calibrated Profile Assumption

Current profile proxy is `e2r_2_1_stock_web_calibrated_proxy`; rollback reference is `e2r_2_0_baseline_reference`. This loop does not re-prove the global Stage2 bonus, Yellow threshold, Green strictness, or price-only blowoff guard. It stress-tests a narrower C19 residual: **brand/retail inventory and margin stories must be separated into three drawers**.

1. True C19 positive: sell-through, inventory normalization, markdown control, operating margin, and FCF/cost bridge move together.
2. Cleanup-only false positive: inventory depletion or channel restructuring exists, but it is still wound dressing rather than a new rerating engine.
3. 4B/4C margin break: discount-channel mix, SG&A pressure, brand exit, or OP collapse overwhelms any revenue or portfolio narrative.

The metaphor for this canonical is a store room. Revenue headline is the front window; inventory quality is what is stacked behind the curtain. If the back room is stuffed with old product, the front window can still sparkle while the margin engine is already coughing.

## 8. Evidence Split / Research Notes

### 8.1 Positive control — quiet margin machinery beats bright windows
Gamsung, Xexymix/Brand X, and Hanssem are useful positives because the route is not just “brand is popular.” The relevant bridge is repeated store/channel expansion, high-priced mix and discount discipline, or cost-efficiency-driven operating-profit repair. In C19, a quiet margin bridge is worth more than a loud revenue headline.

### 8.2 Counterexample control — record growth can still be peak-risk
F&F is the cleanest warning. Record sales and operating profit can make raw components look like Yellow, but if the entry is already priced as peak brand/channel growth and inventory/sell-through durability is not separately verified, the later drawdown can be much larger than the initial upside.

### 8.3 Licensed-IP and direct-store risk — brand familiarity is not a moat by itself
The Nature Holdings shows that a familiar licensed IP can still fail if fixed costs and direct-store overseas execution risk absorb the brand story. C19 needs cost-to-channel survival evidence, not only brand vocabulary.

### 8.4 4B/4C routing — do not let relief rallies reopen broken margin stories
Shinsegae International and Hansome split the protection rules. SI is hard 4C because brand contract termination and OP collapse are thesis breaks even when a short relief rally appears. Hansome is 4B guard because discount-channel mix and OP decline block new Actionable credit but do not necessarily prove permanent thesis death.
