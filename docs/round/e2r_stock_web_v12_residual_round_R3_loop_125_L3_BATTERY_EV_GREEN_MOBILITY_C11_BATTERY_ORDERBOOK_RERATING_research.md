## 9. Archetype Compression Notes

### 9.1 Positive compression

The clean C11 route is not “battery equipment company + order backlog sentence.” It is closer to a factory conveyor belt: an order enters at one end, but calibration should wait until it can see delivery timing, revenue recognition, and gross/operating margin coming off the other end. CIS 2023/2025 and PNT 2024 show that the model should reward **actual conversion** more than raw backlog size.

### 9.2 Counterexample compression

The failure route appears in four flavors:

1. **Sector backlog proxy**: TSI inherits a positive sector chart but lacks a named PO/revenue/margin bridge.
2. **Orderbook exists but high-MAE path remains**: mPLUS has orderbook evidence, yet battery capex timing and delivery path argue for staged entry.
3. **Product optionality boundary**: Daejoo has important silicon-anode technology, but it is not a C11 orderbook row unless customer/order/revenue conversion appears.
4. **Reprice/segment trap**: Jeil M&S and Avaco show why IPO heat or display-equipment earnings should not be mapped into battery orderbook credit.


The model should not grant C11 Stage3-Yellow just because a company has an orderbook. It should grant higher credit when the orderbook has already begun to behave like earnings: delivery schedule, revenue recognition, margin bridge, and customer/capex survival. If the evidence is only a sector chart, IPO story, silicon-anode product page, or display-equipment earnings, the row should stay at Stage2/watch or 4B.
