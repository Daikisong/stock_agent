## 7. Case Notes / Residual Diagnosis

### 7.1 Direct customer-capex shock is C10 thesis-break evidence, but not a permanent no-reopen state

The four 2022 rows use the same downcycle regime: customer memory capex was delayed or cut. The 30D/90D paths validate protection: Wonik IPS, TES, Jusung, and Eugene Tech all suffered immediate drawdown after the capex-cut evidence. But the 180D path partially recovered as the market began to anticipate 2H23 improvement. Therefore the C10 rule should not be a one-way guillotine. It should be **hard 4C/watch during the capex shock**, followed by a mandatory **reopen clock** when customer capex/order/revenue conversion returns.

### 7.2 Profile-only equipment exposure after a rerating is not Stage2-Actionable

The 2024-07-16 late-extension block is the cleanest 4B cluster in this file. Hana Materials, PSK, GST, and KCTech all had plausible semiconductor equipment/materials exposure. Yet the evidence was either product-profile exposure, utilization headline, or stale sector beta, not a fresh customer order. The stock-web paths show low 180D upside and deep drawdown. The correct action is local/full Stage4B watch, not Stage2-Actionable and not hard 4C.

### 7.3 Reopen rows after reset should not be blocked by prior 4B/4C

The 2025-01-14 reopen rows use a different condition: the customer capex clock reset toward 2025 DRAM 1b/TSV and advanced NAND investment. TES, Eugene Tech, GST, and Hana Materials all show positive 180D MFE with manageable MAE compared with the July 2024 late-extension block. This argues for a C10-specific reset gate: prior 4B/4C can clear to Stage2-Actionable when new customer capex clock plus supplier exposure exists, but Green remains blocked until supplier order/revenue/margin conversion is explicit.
