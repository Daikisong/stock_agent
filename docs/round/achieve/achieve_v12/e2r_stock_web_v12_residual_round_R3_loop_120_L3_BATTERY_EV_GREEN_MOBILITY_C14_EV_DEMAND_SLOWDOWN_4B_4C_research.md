## 5. Case Interpretation

### 5.1 What C14 should learn

C14 is not a single red button. It has two different mechanisms that look similar in news headlines but behave differently in calibration. A generic EV-growth slowdown is a yellow warning lamp: it should block aggressive Stage3/Green interpretation only when evidence remains soft. Utilization collapse, explicit capacity/call-off language, repeated operating losses, or fixed-cost deleveraging are a different animal; those are hard-4C evidence because the business model itself has stopped converting orderbook or capacity into margin.

### 5.2 Residual error found

The residual error is not that the global hard-4C route is wrong. The route is correct. The residual error is the sector-specific trigger boundary: in battery materials and EV cells, the phrase 'EV slowdown' can mean anything from a temporary demand pause to a broken utilization thesis. If a future scorer maps every EV slowdown headline straight to Stage4C, it will over-block cases like LGES 2024-01-26. If it waits for formal annual losses, it will be late on SKIET and the cathode-material collapse rows.

### 5.3 Proposed canonical shadow rule

```text
canonical_archetype_rule_candidate = C14_STAGED_4B_TO_4C_UTILIZATION_CALL_OFF_GATE

Rule:
1. Generic EV demand slowdown, lithium/ASP pressure, or softer revenue guidance -> Stage4B / watch, not hard 4C.
2. Stage4C requires at least one hard non-price break: utilization collapse, call-off/order cut, explicit capacity-plan slowdown, repeated operating loss, fixed-cost deleveraging, or management statement that expansion pace is being reduced because of EV demand.
3. Hard 4C is a long-positive block / thesis-break label, not a short signal. POSCO Future M and EcoPro BM both had meaningful rebound MFE after hard evidence; the label protects from Green, but it should not imply immediate one-way downside.
4. For cell majors with continuing investment and diversified demand routes, keep Stage4B until utilization/call-off evidence appears.
```
