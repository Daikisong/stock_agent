## 3. Human-readable conclusion

C11 should behave like an equipment maker's delivery ledger, not like a sector buzzword bucket. A real C11 positive has four links: **named customer or process role → executable order/backlog → delivery/revenue recognition → margin or FCF conversion**. A-PRO clears the first two links and produced a strong MFE path, so it deserves Stage2-Actionable with a local 4B overlay after the fast reprice. NSYS shows that product-role recognition can be early and tradable, but without a named order and margin bridge it should remain below clean Yellow.

The counterexamples are the important part. V-One Tech had a real LG Electronics battery inspection-system contract, but the order scale and conversion quality were too weak for rerating. DENT, Daebo Magnetic, CowinTech, and NainTech show the common failure mode: the company is genuinely in the battery-equipment orbit, but the trigger only proves product identity or sector beta. ONEJOON shows the optionality trap: customer names and solid-state equipment language do not equal current executable backlog.
