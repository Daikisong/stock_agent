## 3. Core finding

C12 should not convert every named-customer contract, localized capacity, or battery-supply-chain headline into Stage3. The mechanism has to cross four small bridges, like a shipment leaving the factory gate rather than only being drawn on a map:

```text
customer contract / localized capacity / customer supply narrative
  -> explicit delivery or shipment timing
  -> revenue recognition and ASP / margin bridge
  -> call-off, utilization, inventory and demand-risk check
  -> only then Stage3-Yellow / Green eligibility
```

Solus and SKC show why the contract path cannot be ignored: low early MAE or named-customer supply can produce meaningful MFE. LG Chem and L&F show the other side: long-dated contracts and ASP/margin pressure leave the holder exposed before shipment conversion. SKIET and WCP are the guardrail pair: once customer demand, utilization, competition, or separator economics break, C12 should become Stage4B or Stage4C rather than remain a hopeful Stage2.
