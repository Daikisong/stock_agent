## 1. Current Calibrated Profile Assumption

This run assumes the v12 rolling profile has already learned the broad global guards: Stage2 needs a bridge, price-only blowoff is not positive evidence, full 4B requires non-price evidence, and hard 4C needs a real thesis break. The purpose here is not to re-prove those global rules. The residual question is narrower: within `C15_MATERIAL_SPREAD_SUPERCYCLE`, when does a raw-material or spread headline actually travel into issuer-level margin/cash, and when is it only weather passing over the roof?

## 2. Round / Large Sector / Canonical Archetype Scope

`C15_MATERIAL_SPREAD_SUPERCYCLE` maps to `R4 / L4_MATERIALS_SPREAD_RESOURCE`. The selected fine-archetype is spread freshness, inventory lag, and pass-through. This loop deliberately mixes chemical, fertilizer, steel pipe/special steel, copper rod, and wire/cable rows because they share the same mechanical question: input cost and product price move at different speeds, while inventory and demand decide whether the spread reaches the income statement.

## 13. Stage2 / Yellow / Green Comparison

- Stage2-watch is appropriate for broad commodity or raw-material exposure when issuer pass-through is not yet proven.
- Stage2-Actionable is appropriate when the source identifies a direct customer/product route or a concrete spread bridge.
- Stage3-Yellow requires either fresh margin/product-mix conversion or a clean reset price phase.
- Stage3-Green should require realized margin/cash plus forward spread persistence. In this loop no row earns a full Green; the strong rows still carry post-peak drawdown risk.

## 14. 4B Local vs Full-window Timing Audit

`001430` is the clean 4B row: the source explicitly reports ASP decline, spread contraction, volume weakness, and inventory valuation loss. `005010`, `024840`, and `025860` are not hard 4C rows; they are Stage cap / watch rows. A spread cycle can punish entries without proving that the company is permanently broken. High MAE is smoke; hard 4C needs proof of the fire.

## 15. Sector-Specific Rule Candidate

`L4_MATERIALS_SPREAD_RESOURCE` evidence should split commodity weather, product price stickiness, inventory-cost lag, demand/pass-through, realized margin/cash conversion, and price phase. A raw material headline is only the gauge; calibration should check whether the pressure reaches the issuer's income statement.

## 16. Canonical-Archetype Rule Candidate

`C15_MATERIAL_SPREAD_SUPERCYCLE` should use a spread-freshness and pass-through ladder: **raw material movement → product price/pass-through → demand or customer route → realized margin/cash → price-phase sanity**. Raw-material exposure without pass-through stays Stage2-watch. Result-only rows after rerating are capped below Green. Inventory-lag damage routes to 4B-watch before hard 4C unless repeated non-price thesis damage confirms death.
