## 4. RedTeam interpretation

### 4.1 4B is a hot brake, not a thesis death sentence

The C20 and C08 late-headline rows show the classic 4B problem. Evidence was not fake: Silicon2, Clio, QRT, and medical-device/platform names had real business vocabulary. The error came from treating **late visible growth** as a fresh entry. When `MFE90 < 20` and `MAE90 <= -35`, R13 should force `Stage4B-Watch` or `Stage2-Watch`, not allow another Stage2-Actionable promotion.

### 4.2 4C must distinguish broken thesis from valuation-reset shock

The C17 reset rows are the mirror image. LG Chem and Hanwha Solutions had loss/oversupply headlines, but the 180D path opened large MFE after a reset. In those rows, a hard 4C at the headline date would cut after the fire already burned through the house and before the rebuild started. R13 should mark these as `Stage4C-Watch / false-break audit` unless the non-price thesis is irreversibly broken.

### 4.3 High MAE is not automatically wrong if the bridge is hard

C25 Curexo and C08 Doosan Tesna show why a blunt MAE block is too crude. Curexo had a deep early drawdown but later produced meaningful MFE when the regulatory/distributor bridge mattered. Doosan Tesna had a clean customer/test leverage path. R13 should prefer staged entry and sizing guards over a hard block when `MFE180` compensates for early volatility and the bridge is specific.
