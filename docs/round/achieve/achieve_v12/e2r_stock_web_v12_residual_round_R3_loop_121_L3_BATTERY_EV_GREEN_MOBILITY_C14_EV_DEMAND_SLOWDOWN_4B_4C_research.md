## 5. Case Interpretation

### 5.1 Residual boundary this loop tests

C14 is a severity split, not a synonym for all battery weakness. The same sector headline can mean three different things: a soft demand pause that should only cap Yellow/Green, a post-MFE reprice that should become local/full 4B, or a real hard-4C thesis break where utilization, customer demand, stock rebalancing, or repeated operating losses break the capacity-to-margin bridge. This loop deliberately adds survivor rows and hard-break rows together so the scorer does not turn C14 into a one-way red button.

### 5.2 What the five representative rows teach

- **WCP / 393890** is the clean hard-4C extension: customer stock rebalancing and reduced European client demand map to deep 90D/180D MAE. This is the evidence standard for `hard_4c_thesis_break_routes_to_4c` in separator names.
- **EcoPro BM / 247540** is a staged 4B example: inventory/ASP loss risk existed, but the path still had meaningful MFE before the later loss confirmation. Use Stage4B first, then hard 4C only after repeated loss or explicit capacity/call-off deterioration.
- **SKC / 011790** shows that a real copper-foil contract can still need a 4B ramp guard after initial MFE. Contract reality is not enough; utilization, ramp timing, and margin absorption must survive.
- **Solus / 336370** is the overblocking counterexample: customer diversification and new shipment route with controlled MAE should prevent a generic EV slowdown label from becoming hard 4C.
- **POSCO Future M / 003670** is the pre-slowdown order-strength counterexample: the LGES supply bridge produced very large MFE. The correct rule is post-peak 4B discipline, not early hard 4C.

### 5.3 Proposed canonical shadow rule

```text
canonical_archetype_rule_candidate = C14_UTILIZATION_CALLOFF_SEVERITY_SPLIT_WITH_SURVIVOR_REOPEN_GATE

Rule:
1. Generic EV demand slowdown / ASP pressure / policy uncertainty -> Stage4B watch or Stage2 cap. Do not route to hard 4C by wording alone.
2. Hard Stage4C requires non-price severity: utilization collapse, reduced customer demand, customer stock rebalancing/call-off, repeated operating losses, fixed-cost deleveraging, or formal capacity-plan slowdown.
3. Survivor/reopen evidence exists when customer diversification, supply-volume bridge, controlled MAE, or large named contract still converts into shipments. Those rows can remain Stage2-Actionable but need post-MFE 4B discipline.
4. A hard 4C label is a long-positive block / thesis-break label, not a one-way short signal.
```
