## 3. Thesis

C12 is not “battery exposure.” It is the pressure test between a signed-or-implied customer route and actual customer pull. A supplier can sit right beside Samsung SDI, LGES, SK On, or a North American JV and still fail C12 if the next link does not appear: call-off cadence, shipping/billing volume, utilization recovery, ASP pass-through, and operating-margin survival.

This loop therefore splits three routes:

```text
Route A: customer program starts + supplier role is direct -> Stage2-Actionable, but staged entry/4B overlay remains.
Route B: inventory depletion + utilization recovery + new OEM/JV supply -> 4B decay into Stage2 reopen.
Route C: precursor/component/material identity without pull-through -> Stage4B or Stage2-watch proxy block.
```

## 5. Case notes

### 5.1 Reopen positives

- `020150 / LOTTE Energy Materials` is the cleanest C12 reopen row. The company explicitly tied expected Q2 profitability improvement to proactive inventory reduction, gradual utilization recovery after customer inventory depletion, and new supply to North American OEMs and customer JVs. The 180D price path then produced +130.07% MFE with only -11.34% MAE. This is not a generic copper-foil headline; it is inventory depletion turning into customer pull.
- `005070 / Cosmo Advanced Materials` remains lower-confidence than Lotte because the evidence is more product/capacity-route than same-day call-off. Still, after the 2024 drawdown, the 2025-03-31 path gives +71.84% 180D MFE. C12 should allow staged reopen, not Green.

### 5.2 Cylindrical supplier positives

- `091580 / Sangsin EDP` and `243840 / Shinheung SEC` are not direct cell makers, but Samsung SDI's 46-series production start is a named customer-program event, and the supplier role is directly tied to cans/caps. The price path supports Stage2-Actionable, especially for Sangsin EDP. The guardrail is that this still needs billing/margin confirmation before Yellow/Green.

### 5.3 Proxy and 4B guards

- `002710 / TCC Steel` and `006110 / Sama Aluminium` show why the upstream component layer needs a discount. The materials matter, but C12 should not over-translate component identity into customer call-off. TCC Steel's 90D MAE was -27.19% and its 180D MFE stayed only +6.38%; Sama Aluminium later recovered but first drew down -28.37%.
- `450080 / EcoPro Materials` is a precursor-identity counterexample. The company is strategically important in high-nickel precursor supply, but the entry row behaved like a post-reprice trap: MFE_180D only +6.35% versus MAE_180D -57.04%. Precursor identity is not customer pull-through.
