## 6. score-return alignment and current-profile stress

C13 behaves like a factory pipe network. A site, product route, JV, funding headline, or named product tells us a pipe exists. The backtest asks whether water moved through the pipe: final customer/order route, utilization/calloff, ex-credit margin, and cash conversion. In this loop, route-only rows split sharply: after a deep reset, direct electrolyte/precursor routes could work; after a rerating or during a utilization loss, the same language failed badly.

### residual pattern
- `450080` and `025900` show why route evidence should not be discarded after reset: when price phase improves and route is direct, Stage2-Actionable can be early enough.
- `278280` provides both sides: low-utilization inventory-loss row is a 4B/counterexample, while later reset-plus-shipments behaves as an Actionable recovery bridge.
- `006110`, `393890`, and `001570` show the trap: product capability or battery narrative without calloff/utilization/cash conversion should not inherit Green or durable Yellow credit.
