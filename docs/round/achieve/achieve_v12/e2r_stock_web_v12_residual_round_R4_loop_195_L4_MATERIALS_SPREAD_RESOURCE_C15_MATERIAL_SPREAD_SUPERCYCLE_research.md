## 4. Main finding

C15 cannot treat every material-price, record-profit, or spread-recovery headline as a fresh supercycle. The second pass shows four distinct pipes inside the same factory:

1. **True product-spread conversion**: TCC Steel in early 2023 had product-cycle evidence and a clean operating-profit bridge, producing extreme MFE.
2. **Late record-profit trap**: SeAH Steel Holdings and Husteel record-profit headlines came after much of the pipe spread cycle had already been priced.
3. **Proxy blowoff**: Choil Aluminum and Namsun Aluminum reacted to aluminium-price headlines but lacked company-level pass-through/margin evidence.
4. **False hard-4C risk**: A product-price decline or rebar-demand warning can be real, but if valuation reset and backlog/export bridge keep MAE contained, it should remain Watch rather than irreversible 4C.

```text
loop_contribution_label: canonical_archetype_rule_candidate

existing_axis_weakened: hard_4c_thesis_break_routes_to_4c_should_not_fire_on_product_price_decline_without_forward_margin_confirmation
```

Rule in plain words: C15 Stage2-Actionable requires **product spread / metal-gain / product mix evidence** plus a **company-level conversion bridge** such as margin, shipment, backlog, pricing pass-through, or revenue timing. A record-profit print after a multi-month rerating is not fresh Stage2. Macro metal price spikes without pass-through should stay Stage2-Watch or false-positive. Product-price declines should not become hard 4C until margin/run-rate or order deterioration confirms the break.
