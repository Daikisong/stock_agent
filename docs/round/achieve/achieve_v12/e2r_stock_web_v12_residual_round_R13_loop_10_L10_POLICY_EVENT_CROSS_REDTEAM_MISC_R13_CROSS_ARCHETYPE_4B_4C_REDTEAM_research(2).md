## 1. Selection rationale

Most conversation-local Priority 0 floor passes have now been filled, and the recent Priority 1 passes pushed the main semicap, battery, backlog, and memory-cycle families toward their local 50-row operating bands. At this point, another same-sector expansion would mostly add volume. The higher-leverage move is to inspect the seam where v12 still leaks: **Local 4B, full 4B, and hard 4C are separated by the shape of the price path, not by the size of the early MFE alone.**

This R13 checkpoint therefore reuses previously materialized local v12 rows whose 30D/90D/180D MFE and MAE were already recorded from stock-web shard paths, and asks a single question: when a row has a strong MFE but also a deep MAE, should it be allowed as full 4B / Green, capped as local 4B, or routed to 4C watch?

## 3. Cross-archetype boundary mechanism

The mechanism is a brake system, not an engine. C02, C07, C11, C14, C20, C24, and C30 may have completely different stories, but the same path defect appears again and again:

```text
large early MFE
  -> narrative labels it as structural confirmation
  -> later MAE reveals missing bridge, delayed thesis break, or price-only blowoff
  -> profile should have capped it as local 4B / Stage2 review / 4C watch
```

A true full 4B should survive both tests: non-price bridge and tolerable path risk. If the row has only price heat and later drawdown, it behaves like a sparkler: bright enough to see, too hot to hold.
