## 14. Stage2 / Yellow / Green Comparison

No Stage3-Green row is used as a hindsight label. Yellow rows are used only when the margin/revenue conversion was observable before or at entry. Stage2-Actionable is allowed for backlog visibility if it has a named customer, visible delivery timing, and margin/revenue bridge. Otherwise Stage2 should stay Watch.

## 15. 4B Local vs Full-window Timing Audit

HD현대마린솔루션 IPO-day row is the cleanest 4B event-cap example. The business quality was real, but listing-day premium already capitalized too much of the future. In contrast, the 2025 reset row shows why 4B should be an overlay rather than a permanent block when revenue/OP conversion arrives later.

## 16. 4C Protection Audit

No hard 4C is proposed. The counterexamples are Stage2 false positives or 4B/event-cap problems, not irreversible thesis breaks. C01 hard 4C should require cancelled orders, delivery collapse, margin bridge failure, or trust/accounting break.

## 17. Sector-Specific Rule Candidate

`L1_COMPONENT_BACKLOG_MARGIN_CONVERSION_AND_EVENT_CAP_GATE_V3`

L1 industrial component suppliers should require two bridges before Stage2-Actionable: a commercial bridge, such as named customer/order/backlog/delivery timing, and a conversion bridge, such as margin, revenue, revision, OPM, or FCF path. IPO/event-premium entries get local 4B guard even when the underlying business is high quality.

## 18. Canonical-Archetype Rule Candidate

`C01_COMPONENT_BACKLOG_MARGIN_CONVERSION_AND_STAGED_ENTRY_GATE_V3`

C01 should not treat all backlog as equal. The archetype splits into: clean conversion, high-MAE success requiring staged entry, and backlog/capacity vocabulary false positive. Stage2-Actionable opens only when backlog visibility and conversion bridge are both present.
