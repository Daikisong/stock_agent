## 6. Residual Interpretation

The common error is not that Stage2 appears too early. Stage2 is the scout. The error is letting a single medical headline dress itself as a full commercial bridge. Approval, positive endpoint, breakthrough status, distributor announcement, CE/FDA/MFDS clearance, or a license upfront is only the door opening. E2R should ask whether anyone is already walking through it: prescriptions, procedure volume, payer access, distributor launch timing, installed base, consumables, royalties, milestone economics, or recognized revenue.

Hard 4C has the opposite problem. In bio, endpoint failure, futility stop, or risk-benefit rejection can be a clean thesis break. But CRLs driven by inspection/GMP/BIMO/process issues, or program-level failures inside diversified issuers, need an offset check. Otherwise the model welds the red light shut even after resubmission route, partner support, or unrelated revenue bridge reopens the path.

```text
R13 rule candidate: R13_BIO_EVENT_SECOND_BRIDGE_AND_ISSUER_OFFSET_GATE_V1
stage2_preserved_when = direct approval/endpoint/device evidence exists
stage2_actionable_requires = second bridge after the event headline
second_bridge_examples = launch timing, payer/reimbursement, prescription/procedure volume, installed base, consumables, royalty/milestone economics, revenue conversion
stage3_green_blocked_until = realized commercial/revenue bridge visible beyond event headline
hard_4c_allowed_when = endpoint/futility/risk-benefit failure or regulator-confirmed clinical thesis break with weak offset
hard_4c_softened_to_4b_when = process CRL, resubmission route, diversified issuer offset, partner support, or non-clinical defect
```
