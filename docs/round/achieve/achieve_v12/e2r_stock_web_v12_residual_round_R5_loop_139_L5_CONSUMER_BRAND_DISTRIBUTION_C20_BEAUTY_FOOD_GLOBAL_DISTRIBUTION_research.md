## 1. Selection rationale

Priority 0 and Priority 1 buckets have already been covered in this session by earlier local loops. The next highest-value residual path is therefore a Priority 2 quality repair. C20 has 78 representative rows in the static No-Repeat Index, so this loop does not try to add quantity for its own sake. It adds a C20-specific rule candidate around **global distribution / channel repeatability / sell-through / margin bridge**, and it stress-tests whether fast K-beauty and K-food repricing should receive local 4B overlays even when the underlying theme is real.

Round/sector mapping is strict: C18~C20 maps to R5 / L5_CONSUMER_BRAND_DISTRIBUTION. This file therefore avoids the common error of writing a C20 sector study under R13.

## 4. Main finding

C20 is not a simple "K-beauty/K-food went global" bucket. The profitable version has a three-link chain: **global channel expansion → repeat sell-through/reorder or customer concentration quality → margin/FCF conversion**. When only the first link is visible, the stock can still produce a fast local MFE, but the path often turns into a 4B problem: the theme is true, yet the entry has already swallowed the next revision.

This loop therefore proposes a canonical shadow gate:

```text
C20_GLOBAL_DISTRIBUTION_REQUIRES_REPEAT_SELLTHROUGH_AND_MARGIN_BRIDGE_WITH_POST_REPRICE_4B_GATE
```

Interpretation:

```text
Increase Stage2-Actionable credit when global distribution is paired with concrete repeat channel, sell-through, or margin bridge evidence.
Cap Stage3-Yellow/Green when evidence is only export/store/channel optionality.
Attach local 4B when MFE30 is large but incremental margin/reorder evidence has not caught up, especially if MAE90 later exceeds -20%.
```

## 5. Evidence split notes

- **삼양식품** is the cleanest positive control. The trigger was not just a ramen-export headline: 1Q24 had record sales and operating profit, overseas revenue was already 75% of sales, and the U.S./China mainstream-channel route was explicitly visible.
- **한국콜마** is a beauty ODM positive where the early signal was customer-quality and margin-recovery, not only export language. Its later path still required profit-lock discipline after the September peak.
- **농심** proves that global food distribution can work but still needs 4B control. The overseas narrative produced strong MFE, then the path suffered a large post-peak drawdown.
- **코스맥스** had a real earnings surprise, yet the post-June drawdown shows why one-quarter surprise plus K-beauty demand should not automatically become durable Green without repeat-order and margin durability.
- **실리콘투** demonstrates the late-entry overheat form. By July 2024, the global distributor story was already known and the forward path was dominated by drawdown risk.
- **아모레퍼시픽** is a mixed-quality C20 case: Western-region growth was real, but China/duty-free weakness kept red-team risk high.
- **빙그레** is the export-optionality proxy trap: Melona/export growth was visible, but the stock-web path did not confirm a scalable margin bridge.

## 6. Current calibrated profile stress test

```text
before_profile_id = e2r_2_1_stock_web_calibrated_proxy
after_profile_id = proposed_C20_shadow_profile
rollback_reference_profile_id = e2r_2_0_baseline_reference
```

The current profile's global bridge rules are directionally right, but C20 still needs a sharper distinction between **early distribution-with-margin conversion** and **late theme-reprice without fresh revision**. The error is not that Stage2 is too generous in all consumer export cases. The error is that the same K-beauty/K-food word can mean two different things depending on timing: early channel-to-margin conversion is a rerating embryo; late headline recognition after a vertical move is a 4B seed.
