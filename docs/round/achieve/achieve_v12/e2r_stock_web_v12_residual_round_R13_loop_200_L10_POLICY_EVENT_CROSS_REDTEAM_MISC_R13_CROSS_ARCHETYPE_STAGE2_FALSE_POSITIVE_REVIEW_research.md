## 6. R13 False-Positive Taxonomy

### 6.1 Single headline / product profile cap

Rows such as `RaonSecure`, `IGLOO`, `Kakao`, `Nasmedia`, `GENIANS 2024`, and `Pearl Abyss` show that a stock can later have non-trivial MFE even when the initial evidence is only a product profile, a market-size narrative, a pipeline, or a one-quarter low-base rebound. R13 interpretation: **Stage2 can remain valid, but Stage2-Actionable, Yellow, and Green require a second bridge.**

### 6.2 Direct bridge with high MAE

Rows such as `Samsung SDS`, `Hyundai AutoEver`, `AhnLab`, `Douzone Bizon`, `NAVER`, `Cafe24`, `CJ ENM`, and `Devsisters` show issuer-level revenue/OP/GMV/IP monetization evidence, but several have deep 90D/180D MAE. R13 interpretation: **do not delete Stage2-Actionable when a direct bridge exists; use high MAE as a Yellow/Green escalation brake.**

### 6.3 One-off hit / repeatability risk

`Devsisters`, `CJ ENM`, `SM Entertainment`, and `Pearl Abyss` show the content/IP version of the same issue: a hit title, comeback calendar, drama slate, or pipeline can be a valid Stage2 marker, but not a repeat-monetization proof. R13 interpretation: **repeat revenue, live-service retention, paid-subscriber path, royalty/licensing economics, concert/MD revenue, or realized OP conversion is required before escalation.**

### 6.4 4B before 4C

`EMRO`, `Cafe24 2025`, and `Pearl Abyss` show that even a negative or overextended evidence row does not automatically become hard 4C. R13 interpretation: **4B/watch is the first stop unless there is non-price thesis break plus weak offset quality.**
