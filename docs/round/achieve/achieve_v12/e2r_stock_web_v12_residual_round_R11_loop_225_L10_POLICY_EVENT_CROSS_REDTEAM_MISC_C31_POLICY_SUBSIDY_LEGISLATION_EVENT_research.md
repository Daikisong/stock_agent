## 6. Current profile stress test

Fuel-tax rollback is a deceptively clean policy headline. It looks like a direct price pass-through event, but in listed equities it often behaves like a valve rather than a pump. The tax decision can move pump prices or LPG butane retail prices; it does not automatically prove that a listed refiner, LPG importer, distributor, or petroleum reseller has captured incremental margin or cash. The cases split into three buckets.

First, `018670` and `003650` show survivable price paths. They can justify Stage2-Actionable because MAE was controlled and 180D MFE was meaningful. Still, the evidence should not jump to Stage3-Green unless margin/cash conversion appears.

Second, `017940`, `024060`, and `004090` are the key false-positive repairs. They are policy-sensitive names, but the 90D drawdown was large enough that a policy headline without issuer-level margin evidence must be capped into local 4B-watch.

Third, `010950`, `078930`, and `014530` show why broad oil/refining exposure is not the same as C31 cashflow finality. Their later price paths may partly work, but the route is not clean enough to score as a direct policy-subsidy/tax-cashflow conversion.

## 7. Shadow rule candidate

### Sector-specific rule candidate

`L10 policy/tax rows should split public policy existence, commodity/pump-price pass-through, issuer-level margin bridge, cash conversion, and price phase. A tax headline is the lever; issuer margin and cash are the wheel actually turning.`

### Canonical-archetype rule candidate

`C31 should add a fuel-tax pass-through ladder: tax-cut rollback headline -> issuer route -> inventory/product spread or margin bridge -> cash conversion -> phase sanity. Fuel-tax rollback can start Stage2-watch. Stage2-Actionable requires an issuer-specific route and tolerable MAE. Yellow/Green require direct margin/cash evidence. High-MAE proxy rows become 4B-watch, not automatic hard 4C, unless demand or margin route death is confirmed.`
