## 4. Core research question
High MAE is not a single animal. It can be a storm the thesis survives, or a sinkhole the thesis falls into. The model should not treat every -20% to -50% drawdown as one same warning label. This loop separates three buckets:

1. `absorbable_high_mae`: the bridge is still alive and later upside compensates volatility.
2. `non_absorbable_late_result_or_policy_trap`: the evidence is stale, generic, or after the local peak; high MAE is not paid back by forward MFE.
3. `confirmed_thesis_death_4c`: non-price thesis break is visible and 4C should fire early, because remaining upside is tiny and drawdown is large.

## 9. Rule candidate
### Canonical archetype-specific rule candidate
`R13_CROSS_ARCHETYPE_HIGH_MAE_GUARDRAIL` should add an absorption-vs-thesis-death ladder. If the evidence bridge is still direct and later MFE compensates the early drawdown, cap at Yellow/Watch rather than hard 4C. If the evidence is result-only, policy-only, or peak-zone without issuer cashflow/utilization conversion, high MAE should cap the row below Green. If non-price thesis death is confirmed, classify as hard 4C even before the full accounting collapse becomes obvious.
