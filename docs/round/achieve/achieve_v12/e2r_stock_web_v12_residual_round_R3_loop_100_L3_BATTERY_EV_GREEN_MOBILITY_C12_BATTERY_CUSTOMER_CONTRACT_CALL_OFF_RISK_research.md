### Specific answers required by v12
1. Current calibrated profile is directionally correct on the global Stage2 bridge, but C12 still needs a sharper named-customer / delivery / revenue-recognition gate before Stage3-Yellow.
2. Actual MFE/MAE confirms C12 is an asymmetric high-MAE archetype: four of six rows had 180D MAE worse than -32%, while only the named Samsung SDI cathode contract had a clean low-MAE success path.
3. The Stage2-Actionable bonus should remain available for disclosed customer contracts, but it should be capped when delivery timing is long, customer identity is undisclosed, contract scale is small relative to market cap, or revenue recognition is delayed.
4. Yellow threshold 75 is not the main issue. The leak occurs when contract_score and backlog_visibility_score cross into Yellow before delivery/revenue/margin conversion is visible.
5. Green threshold 87/revision 55 should remain strict. None of the equipment-contract rows warrants Stage3-Green at trigger.
6. Price-only blowoff guard remains appropriate. Cowintech, Wonik PNE, Daebo Magnetic, and GI Tech show how local MFE can reverse into full-window high-MAE without a confirmed conversion bridge.
7. Full 4B non-price requirement should be retained and made more C12-specific: long delivery window, undisclosed counterparty, pilot/customer PO without mass-production conversion, or optional new business without recurring revenue should be enough for 4B watch.
8. Hard 4C routing should not trigger from EV slowdown or delivery timing alone. Escalate to 4C only when explicit customer call-off, cancellation, production suspension, or qualification failure is disclosed.

## Proposed shadow rule candidate
```text
C12_CUSTOMER_CONTRACT_REQUIRES_DELIVERY_REVENUE_MARGIN_AND_CALLOFF_RISK_GATE_SECOND_PASS
```

Rule intent:
- Allow Stage2-Actionable for disclosed customer contract, backlog, or formal PO.
- Allow Stage3-Yellow only if at least two of these are visible: named customer quality, delivery schedule with near-term revenue recognition, margin/FCF bridge, repeat-contract pattern, or confirmed mass-production conversion.
- Cap at Stage2-Watch/4B if the trigger is mostly contract size, undisclosed customer, far-out delivery, pilot PO, optional new business, or customer CAPEX dependency.
- Route to hard 4C only after explicit customer call-off, contract cancellation, qualification failure, or production stop.
