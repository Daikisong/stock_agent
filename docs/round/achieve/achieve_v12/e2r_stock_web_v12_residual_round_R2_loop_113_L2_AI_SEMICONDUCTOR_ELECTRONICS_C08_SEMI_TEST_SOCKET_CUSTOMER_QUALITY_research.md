## 3. Residual Mechanism

C08 is not “any semiconductor stock with HBM vocabulary.” It is a narrow mechanism: customer qualification, test socket acceptance, repeat consumable demand, outsourced test utilization, and margin conversion. When those gears mesh, a small socket/test company can convert order quality into margin and cash flow. When the row is really C07 equipment order, C09 valuation blowoff, or C10 memory-cycle beta wearing a C08 mask, the profile sees early MFE but later walks into deep MAE.

The 23 rows split into three groups:

1. **Positive C08 bridge**: repeat socket / tester customer acceptance / consumable quality rows where MFE survives at 90D and 180D.
2. **Mixed boundary rows**: meaningful MFE but high MAE or mechanism overlap with equipment/metrology/materials.
3. **Counterexamples**: price-only socket/test labels, OSAT beta, or equipment-cycle contaminants that should remain Stage2 or Local-4B-Watch.
