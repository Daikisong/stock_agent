## 6. Residual Findings

### 6.1 Offset quality decides 4B vs hard 4C

Ugly evidence alone is not enough. Weak quarter, utilization decline, net-profit compression, demand slowdown, product-profile exposure, or approval/event disappointment should first route to `Stage4B/watch` unless the non-price thesis break is confirmed and offset quality is weak.

Confirmed hard 4C evidence remains protected when the issue is core thesis failure: manufacturing/clinical rejection requiring a new clinical route, program failure, persistent utilization collapse, order cancellation, liquidity/trust break, or a customer/capacity route that has stopped converting.

### 6.2 Stage2 reopen requires a second bridge

A damaged thesis can reopen to Stage2-Actionable when direct evidence appears: order, shipment, utilization rebound, revenue conversion, cost-rate or OP bridge, customer route, reimbursement/procedure route, or realized margin/cashflow conversion.

But single-variable evidence remains capped. Product page exposure, broad memory beta, strategic-resource policy label, AI medical device designation, or distribution announcement without realized conversion should not become Yellow/Green.

### 6.3 High MAE is a brake, not a delete key

Rows with direct bridge and high forward MFE can still have deep MAE. The correct interpretation is not to delete Stage2-Actionable, but to block Stage3-Yellow/Green until execution repeats and cashflow/margin/procedure/order conversion becomes visible.
