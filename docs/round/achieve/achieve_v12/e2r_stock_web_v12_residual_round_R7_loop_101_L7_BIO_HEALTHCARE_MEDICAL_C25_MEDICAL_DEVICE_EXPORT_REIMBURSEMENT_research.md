## Interpretation

C25 behaves differently from plain bio-event C23/C24. Medical-device export/reimbursement rerating should not unlock Yellow or Green just because the company has a device label, export narrative, China/US channel wording, or reimbursement optionality. The path is closer to a three-valve pipeline: **procedure volume → distributor/customer acceptance → revenue/margin bridge**. If the second valve is missing, pressure builds in the share price first and then leaks through MAE.

Positive paths in this loop are not purely price-led. `214150`, `226400`, `060280`, and `302550` show that C25 can rerate when a device export/procedure-volume bridge appears after a reset or when adoption converts into a low-MAE path. Counterexamples `145720`, `156100`, `261200`, and `100120` show that device/export/reimbursement labels alone are not enough; the price path either failed to generate useful MFE or suffered MAE below -30% to -57% within 180 trading days.

## Current calibrated profile stress test

- `stage2_required_bridge` is directionally correct but should become more explicit for C25: require export/procedure/reimbursement-to-revenue/margin bridge, not merely export addressable market.
- `local_4b_watch_guard` remains useful. C25 false positives often had a local event or label spike before demand/procedure proof arrived.
- `hard_4c_confirmation` needs a C25 subtype: for dental/spine/imaging/device companies, large MAE is not enough; the 4C trigger should be tied to failed customer pull, procedure-volume fade, reimbursement delay, distributor inventory, or revenue/margin bridge break.
- `source_proxy_only` rows are calibration-usable for price path stress but blocked for promotion until URL repair.
