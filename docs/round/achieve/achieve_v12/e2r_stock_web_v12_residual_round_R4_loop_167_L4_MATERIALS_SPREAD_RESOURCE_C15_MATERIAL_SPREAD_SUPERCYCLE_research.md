## 3. Evidence spine

- **Early steel-price spread signal:** POSCO and Hyundai Steel moved best when steel-price increases and demand recovery were visible before the final record earnings headline.
- **Late peak-earnings trap:** POSCO 2021-07-09 and Hyundai Steel 2021-04-27 show that reported black-turn or record-profit prints can arrive after the share price already consumed the cycle.
- **2024 weak-demand regime:** POSCO, Hyundai Steel, SeAH Besteel and Dongkuk Steel show that a price hike or bottom-call is not enough when raw-material cost, imports, construction drag, or end-market weakness dominates.
- **Copper/coated-steel reset:** Pungsan's copper metal-gain setup worked only with an exit guard; KG Steel's post-reset coated-steel setup was cleaner because pricing/export/capital-return bridges appeared after valuation compression.

## 6. Residual diagnosis

C15 should not be treated as a generic “materials up” bucket. It needs an **early spread confirmation and late-cycle exit gate**:

1. **Early cycle:** when steel/copper product prices are rising, demand recovery is visible, and the trigger arrives before final record-profit confirmation, Stage2-Actionable is allowed. POSCO 2021-01-22 is the clean example.
2. **Late cycle:** when the evidence is a record-profit print or black-turn report after months of rerating, the same evidence becomes a 4B/high-MAE guard. POSCO 2021-07-09 and Hyundai Steel 2021-04-27 show this inversion.
3. **Weak-demand regime:** in 2024, price increases and spread previews did not offset raw-material costs, weak construction demand, low-priced imports, and volume fragility. These rows block Stage2 even when one quarter looks better.
4. **Reset exception:** after valuation reset, a smaller but cleaner bridge—pricing, export drive, product mix, and capital return—can work, as in KG Steel 2025-02-12.
