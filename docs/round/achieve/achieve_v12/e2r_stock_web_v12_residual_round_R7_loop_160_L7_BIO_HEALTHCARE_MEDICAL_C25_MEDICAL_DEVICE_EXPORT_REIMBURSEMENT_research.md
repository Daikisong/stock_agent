## 5. Case notes

### CASE01 — Classys / 214150 — structural success with exit guard

Classys fits C25 because its aesthetic medical device model depends on export product launches, installed-base expansion, and recurring consumable usage. The 2025 guidance/product-launch evidence gave a clean Stage2-Actionable trigger. Stock-Web confirms strong upside from the 2025-02-19 close, but the drawdown after the May peak means the positive row should still carry a C25 4B exit guard rather than automatic Green.

### CASE02 — Dentium / 145720 — dental implant export thesis without enough margin-volume confirmation

Dentium is the mirror image. Dental implant export exposure and China demand/VBP vocabulary can look like a C25 rerating, but the after-close Q1 disappointment led to a next-trading-day entry with only 7.73% 180D MFE and -55.11% 180D MAE. The lesson is that China implant volume and margin confirmation must be treated as a hard bridge, not a narrative bonus.

### CASE03 — Vieworks / 100120 — Q1 OP rebound failed to become a durable C25 rerating

Vieworks had a legitimate Q1 operating-profit rebound and next-generation X-ray detector growth language. But the row produced only 3.82% MFE across 30/90/180D and a -31.67% 180D MAE. C25 should not promote a single-quarter detector rebound unless it is tied to named export orders, reimbursement, or durable detector replacement demand.

### CASE04 — Lunit / 328130 — eventual MFE, unacceptable MAE

The Volpara acquisition was real global medical-AI commercialization evidence, but the price path was not a clean rerating. 30D and 90D MAE reached -21.62% and -45.52% before the later 180D MFE of 50.79%. This is a classic C25 high-MAE success: the structure can be valid, but the trigger needs staged-entry and capital/integration risk guards.

### CASE05 — VUNO / 338220 — AI medical-device commercialization route underweighted

VUNO's DeepCARS/medical-AI device route is a positive C25 row because the evidence connected product status to commercialization/reimbursement-like revenue adoption. The 180D MFE of 45.69% supports the structural signal, while the post-peak drawdown means the rule should still separate positive Stage2 from later 4B exit management.

### CASE06 — JVM / 054950 — pharmacy automation export plus recurring MRO bridge

JVM's 3Q24 evidence gave the type of bridge C25 needs: export share by region, operating profit, pharmacy automation demand, and recurring MRO/pouch economics. The row had an uncomfortable -18.02% MAE but ultimately produced 35.99% 180D MFE, making it a positive row with staged-entry language rather than a simple failed rerating.
