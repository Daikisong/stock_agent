## Main Residual Finding

C18 behaves like a shop with two doors. Through one door, real reorder/renewal pressure walks in: 대상, 풀무원, 코웨이, 쿠쿠홀딩스 showed strong 90D/180D MFE with shallow MAE when the trigger was framed as channel reorder, overseas rental renewal, or food/consumer-durable restocking with margin bridge. Through the other door, the same words — export, overseas, channel, health-functional, food, reorder — can be a painted sign on an empty storefront. CJ제일제당, 콜마비앤에이치, 코스맥스엔비티, 쿠쿠홈시스 show why C18 needs a verified channel bridge before Yellow/Green.

The residual error is not that consumer export labels are useless. The error is letting the label sit in the driver’s seat. In C18, the driver should be one of the following: repeat order, sell-through, rental renewal, inventory normalization, margin bridge, or revenue revision. If those are absent, relative strength is a flare, not a compass.

## Stage / 4B / 4C Audit

Stage3-Green lateness could not be computed because this loop does not include a confirmed Green trigger sequence. The relevant audit is instead Stage2/Yellow admission versus local 4B watch. The four counterexamples show low MFE and deep MAE after entry, especially `222040` with MFE_180D 3.45% / MAE_180D -50.78% and `097950` with MFE_180D 4.62% / MAE_180D -40.31%. These are classic C18 label-spike traps: the theme gets a shop-window display, but reorder and margin fail to enter the ledger.

For this loop, Stage4B should remain local/watch unless there is non-price evidence of inventory/margin/channel deterioration. No full 4B/4C production rule is proposed.
