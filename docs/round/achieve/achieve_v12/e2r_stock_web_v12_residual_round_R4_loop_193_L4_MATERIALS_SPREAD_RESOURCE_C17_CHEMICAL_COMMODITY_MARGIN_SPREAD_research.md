## Case thesis

C17 has a recurring failure mode: **spread vocabulary arrives before spread conversion**. Words like petrochemical recovery, NCC spread bottom, non-China polysilicon premium, PE price improvement, or loss narrowing are not enough. The useful gate is whether the spread signal has crossed the bridge into **segment profit, run-rate shipment, feedstock cost relief, and reset valuation**.

This loop stress-tests two opposite mistakes:

1. **False Stage2**: bottom-call or loss-narrowing language is treated as actionable before segment profit conversion.
2. **False hard 4C**: loss/oversupply headlines are treated as irreversible after the stock has already reset and the next spread regime can produce positive MFE.

## Case notes

### 1. LG Chem 2024 Q1 — loss narrowing was not spread durability

LG Chem's Q1 2024 petrochemical division still showed an operating loss even though the loss narrowed from the prior quarter. The price path was a clean counterexample: MFE_180D was only 2.36%, while MAE_180D reached -41.12%. The current profile should not upgrade loss-narrowing plus cost-cut lag into Stage2-Actionable unless product spread and segment profit conversion are visible.

### 2. LG Chem FY2024 loss headline — false hard 4C after reset

The FY2024 petrochemical oversupply headline looked like a hard thesis break. But the entry after the loss headline had MFE_180D of 91.63% and MAE_180D of -17.87%. This is not a clean Stage2 success; it is a **false-4C reset audit**. Hard 4C should not fire on loss vocabulary alone when the stock has already absorbed the cycle reset.

### 3. Hanwha Solutions Q2 2024 — loss narrowing but no conversion

Q2 2024 had narrower renewable losses and chemical losses, but both businesses still lacked a durable profit bridge. The 180D path capped at 13.73% MFE and fell to -41.73% MAE. This strengthens the C17 rule that loss narrowing is only Stage2-Watch unless it is supported by ASP/run-rate/margin conversion.

### 4. Hanwha Solutions FY2024 loss — reset relief can beat hard 4C

The FY2024 net-loss headline was ugly, yet the subsequent 90D and 180D path delivered 89.98% and 89.98% MFE. The correct action is not immediate bullish promotion; it is a staged reset rule: hard 4C should wait for a second confirmation if price has already reset and operational optionality remains.

### 5. OCI Holdings Q4 2023 — non-China premium narrative without run-rate bridge

OCI Holdings' Q4 2023 presentation gave a non-China polysilicon premium angle, but the same presentation also contained volume and cost/project drags. The entry produced only 12.47% MFE_180D versus -42.45% MAE_180D. This is a textbook C17 false positive: commodity premium narrative without run-rate conversion.

### 6. OCI Holdings FY2024 — optionality after reset

The FY2024 operating profit decline headline would have looked like a negative C17 event. The price path says it was better handled as a reset-entry watch: MFE_180D reached 71.17%, even though MAE_90D still hit -23.9%. This becomes a staged-entry positive with 4B/high-MAE guard, not a clean Green unlock.

### 7. Daehan Yuhwa Q1 2024 — ethylene spread forecast before profit conversion

The Q1 2024 report expected another operating loss and relied on ethylene/NCC spread recovery expectations. The stock briefly opened MFE_90D of 21.05%, but the same row later suffered MAE_180D of -48.57%. Bottom-call evidence before realized spread conversion should stay Stage2-Watch.

### 8. Daehan Yuhwa 2024 Q3 spread recovery — still below normal

By September 2024, NCC spread had moved off the trough but remained below a normal level. The 180D price path capped at 12.6% MFE and fell to -34.73% MAE. This confirms that C17 needs a **spread durability threshold**, not just a direction-of-change signal.

Rule text:

> For C17, Stage2-Actionable requires at least one spread durability bridge and one profit-conversion bridge. Spread durability can be product spread above normal range, feedstock-cost relief, or ASP/cost lag turning favorable. Profit conversion can be segment OP improvement, run-rate shipment recovery, cash margin, or explicit revision. Loss narrowing, bottom-call spread language, or premium vocabulary alone remains Stage2-Watch/local 4B. Conversely, hard 4C should not fire on an ugly loss/oversupply headline if the stock has already reset and forward MFE/MAE shows positive asymmetry.
