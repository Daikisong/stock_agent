## 8. Current-profile residual errors

- Route/product evidence can be real but still insufficient for Green when order finality, margin bridge, and cash conversion are missing.
- High-MAE rows should not all be burned into hard 4C. When the customer/product route survives, the better label is local 4B-watch or position-size cap.
- Direct result/service evidence after a reset can be accepted earlier than a strict Green rule would allow; otherwise the profile becomes too late.

## 9. Sector-specific rule candidate

L1 nuclear/grid service and equipment rows should split route existence, issuer-level order/customer finality, realized margin, working-capital/cash conversion, and price phase. A product route is the pipe; margin and cash are the water moving through it.

## 10. Canonical-archetype rule candidate

C01 should use a route-to-margin/cash phase ladder: route evidence → issuer/customer order finality → margin/cost proof → working-capital/cash conversion → phase sanity. Route-only after rerating is 4B-watch; high MAE with live route is not hard 4C; Green waits for cash freshness.

## 12. 4B / 4C red-team notes

- Local 4B-watch is the right door when an otherwise real route appears after a crowded theme rally.
- Hard 4C requires non-price thesis death: order route cancellation, customer/funding loss, repeated cash-flow damage, or accounting/trust break.
- C01 backlog/order routes should be promoted only when the pipe carries margin/cash; route text alone is just a map, not proof of flow.

## 13. Threshold stress test

The Stage2-Actionable bonus remains useful when the evidence is an issuer-level agreement, service route, or direct IR route after reset. It should not apply equally to company-profile/product-page proxy rows after a rerating. Stage3-Yellow can be justified by direct agreement/result evidence, but Stage3-Green needs realized margin and cash conversion.
