## 4. Interpretation

The 4B/4C boundary behaves like a circuit breaker with two switches, not one. The first switch is **non-price risk evidence**: slowdown, utilization, order conversion failure, inventory loss, margin break, backlog slowdown, or valuation/positioning blowoff. The second switch is **offset quality**: new-model shipments, ESS/non-EV demand, net cash, Q/Q recovery, another segment ramp, or still-intact backlog. A hard 4C should fire only when the first switch is on and the second switch is absent or weak.

- Good 4B rows: TES and Hana Materials show why generic customer capex / memory recovery propagation should be capped as 4B or Green-blocker when supplier-level order conversion is weak.
- Good 4C rows: Samsung SDI 2024 Q3 and S-Oil 2023 weak margin rows show proper hard 4C protection when the operating thesis itself breaks and 180D MFE stays low.
- False hard-4C rows: LGES 2024 Q2, Samsung SDI 2025 Q1, SK Innovation 2023 Q4, and DL E&C 2025 show why offset evidence prevents automatic 4C even when the headline is ugly.
- Local 4B not hard 4C: LS ELECTRIC 2024 Q2 shows a violent local drawdown after price extension, but the backlog / U.S. power-equipment thesis survived enough to avoid full 4C treatment.
