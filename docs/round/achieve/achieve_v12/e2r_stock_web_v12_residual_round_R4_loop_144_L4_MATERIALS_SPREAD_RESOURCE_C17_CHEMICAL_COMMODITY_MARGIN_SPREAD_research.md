## 5. Interpretation by failure mode

### 5.1 Early spread-to-OP bridge works, but it decays quickly

`C17_SOIL_Q1_2022_REFINING_MARGIN_EARLY_STAGE2` and `C17_KUMHO_NB_LATEX_BPA_SPREAD_EARLY_STAGE2` are the two positive controls. S-Oil's Q1 2022 evidence connected tight petroleum-product supply, jet-fuel demand recovery, and strengthening Asian refining margins to earnings. Kumho's March 2021 row connected NB-latex/BPA spreads to earnings revision. Both produced useful 30D/90D upside.

But both positives also show why C17 cannot behave like a long backlog archetype. S-Oil's 30D MFE reached 17.70%, but 180D MAE widened to -26.22%. Kumho's 90D MFE reached 28.11%, but 180D MAE widened to -34.12%. So the rule is not “chemical spread headline equals Green.” The rule is “early spread bridge gets Stage2-Actionable, then the model must watch the spread survival gauge.”

### 5.2 Record-profit headlines are often accounting lag, not fresh evidence

S-Oil's Q2 2022 and Kumho's Q2/FY2021 rows are useful because the evidence was not fake. The companies really did report strong or record profit. The failure is timing: spot margin or demand had already started to normalize. `C17_SOIL_Q2_2022_RECORD_MARGIN_LATE_STAGE3_TRAP` still had a 30D rebound, but the drawdown after the 180D peak was -30.69%. Kumho's Q2 and full-year confirmation rows had almost no forward MFE and deep MAE. This is precisely the current-profile residual error: a general evidence bridge can still overweight lagged accounting confirmation unless C17 parses forward spread commentary.

### 5.3 Inventory loss is the hard 4C separator

`C17_SOIL_Q4_2022_INVENTORY_LOSS_HARD_4C` is the hard 4C row. The source said refining margins remained solid, but inventory losses from falling oil prices broke operating-profit conversion. That distinction matters. If spread language is positive but inventory/cost mechanics break the bridge, the model should not treat the stock as a simple spread rebound candidate.

## 7. Shadow rule candidate

```text
canonical_archetype_rule_candidate = C17_SPREAD_MARGIN_STAGE2_REQUIRES_FORWARD_SPREAD_SURVIVAL_AND_INVENTORY_COST_PASS_THROUGH_GATE

Positive credit condition:
- direct chemical/refining spread indicator exists: NB latex/BPA/PX/synthetic rubber spread, refining margin, lube spread, feedstock-product spread
- evidence links spread to operating profit, revision, or cash flow, not merely revenue
- feedstock cost pass-through is intact
- inventory loss risk is absent or explicitly manageable
- demand guide and utilization do not contradict the spread headline
- signal appears before most of the price move

Decay / 4B condition:
- record profit is backward-looking while spot spread or demand is already falling
- 30D MFE is front-loaded and 90D/180D MAE expands
- source itself mentions raw-material cost pressure, maintenance, reduced spreads, or demand normalization
- reported result confirmation appears after the price peak

Hard 4C condition:
- inventory loss, feedstock spike, demand collapse, or spread compression breaks operating-profit conversion
- management/source says margins remain okay but profit turns to loss because of inventory/cost mechanics
```
