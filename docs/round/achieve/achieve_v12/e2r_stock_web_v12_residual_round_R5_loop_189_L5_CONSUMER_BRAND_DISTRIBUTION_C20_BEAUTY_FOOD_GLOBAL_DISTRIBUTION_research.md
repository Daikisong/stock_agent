## 4. Case Notes

### 4.1 Silicon2 — early platform confirmation versus late headline

The 2024-05-22 Silicon2 row is the clean positive: the evidence already had global distributor structure, record 1Q24 result, non-China offset, and operating margin bridge. The stock delivered strong 30D/90D MFE, but the post-peak drawdown shows C20 needs a local 4B exit guard once platform rerating becomes crowded.

The 2024-08-16 Silicon2 row is the mirror image. Sales and operating profit growth were still spectacular, but the entry was late. Forward MFE was only 5.53% while 90D/180D MAE reached -48.45%. This row blocks “growth headline = fresh Stage2” logic.

### 4.2 Clio — Q1 channel expansion was usable, Q2 record-half was late

Clio's Q1 result had a concrete channel bridge: Japan offline expansion/localization, China category-killer demand, skincare brand profitability, and 63% operating-profit growth. That supported Stage2-Actionable, but the 180D drawdown argues against letting it drift into unguarded Green.

The Q2 record-half row had true sales/OP growth but poor forward price asymmetry. It is a C20 late-entry counterexample, not a new Green unlock.

### 4.3 TonyMoly — one-quarter turnaround versus confirmed reset entry

TonyMoly Q2 had visible sales and OP growth, but forward MFE was limited and MAE was deep. The row should stay Stage2-Watch. By Q3, however, new-channel growth and offline drag offset were clearer, and the reset entry produced large MFE with low MAE. This is the type of post-reset C20 row that current profile can easily mark too late.

### 4.4 APR — global device/cosmetics mix after split

APR is deliberately introduced only after the 2024-10-31 corporate-action candidate. The FY2024 IR evidence shows overseas mix and dual cosmetics/beauty-device engines. The February 2025 reset row is a clean structural success. The March follow-through row is also positive, but it should be treated as staged-entry/high-MAE success rather than a fresh aggressive Green because rerating velocity was already high.

## 5. Score / Return Alignment Notes

- Good C20 rows require **channel/reorder bridge + margin/revision conversion**.
- Bad C20 rows often have true revenue growth but fail because the entry is late, the evidence is already consensus, or the improvement is only one quarter.
- C20 should not treat K-beauty vocabulary, 해외 매출 증가, or record sales as sufficient by themselves.
- A strong distributor/platform row can become a 4B risk quickly after 30D MFE compresses the remaining upside.

## 6. Proposed Shadow Rule

```text
canonical_archetype_rule_candidate = C20_REORDER_CHANNEL_MARGIN_CONVERSION_AND_LATE_HEADLINE_EXIT_GATE_V2
```

Rule candidate:

1. Allow `Stage2-Actionable` when at least one **channel/reorder bridge** and one **margin/revision conversion bridge** are present.
2. Apply `Stage2-Watch` rather than actionable when the row is only a one-quarter turnaround, one-country channel commentary, or generic K-beauty vocabulary.
3. Attach a local `Stage4B-Watch` when the evidence is true but late and forward asymmetry looks like `MFE90 < 15%` with `MAE90 <= -20%`.
4. For post-split/newly listed beauty names, use only clean post-corporate-action windows for calibration.
