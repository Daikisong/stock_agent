R13 is the correct round because this is not a new C01, C30, or C18 sector study. It reuses those source rows only as a holdout corpus and explicitly does **not** mutate source Cxx coverage counts.

## 5. Findings

### 5.1 Valid price rows can still require source repair

Every row in this holdout has the required MFE/MAE fields, but 9 rows rely on news/proxy-type evidence rather than a primary filing, company IR, or detailed research bridge. Those rows should remain usable for red-team and watch logic, but they should not be the sole evidence for Stage2-Actionable or hard 4C.

### 5.2 C30 is the main accounting/trust boundary

C30 rows are closest to a real accounting/trust axis because PF exposure, liquidity, cost-rate, guarantees, and valuation reset can change the thesis. The holdout keeps weak or loss-headline rows in Stage4C-Watch / reset audit unless the issuer-level balance-sheet break is confirmed twice.

### 5.3 C01 and C18 provide control positives and proxy-source counterexamples

C01 hard-bridge positives such as backlog/margin conversion and C18 clean channel/reorder positives preserve the signal. But event-cap, local 4B, and proxy-source rows should be cooled down before promotion. R13 must therefore validate the evidence road as well as the price road.
