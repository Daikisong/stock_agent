## 1. Objective

This R13 loop is not a new sector-positive hunt. It compresses recent source-sector rows from C10, C13, C15, and C31 into a cross-archetype high-MAE holdout. The purpose is to test the guardrail that high MAE should behave like a **stage-escalation brake** rather than a universal bearish deletion rule.

The repeated failure mode is simple: the same evidence that justifies Stage2 or Stage2-Actionable can still produce a painful 30D/90D/180D path. That does not always mean the signal was wrong. It often means the bridge was too early, too single-variable, or too far from cash/margin conversion to permit Yellow or Green.

## 8. Interpretation

### 8.1 What this refresh confirms

High MAE is not the same thing as a failed thesis. In C10, direct tester orders and realized tester revenue can survive as Stage2-Actionable, but the 2024 YC / Exicon rows show why product pipeline or late post-spike contract evidence must remain capped. In C15, cement price pass-through created some operating-profit conversion rows, yet sector-level price-hike language still lacked the durability required for Yellow or Green. In C31, policy or value-up mechanics can be direct evidence, but tariff relief and capital-return plans are still vulnerable to political caps, cycle offset, or implementation lag.

### 8.2 Rule candidate

```text
rule_candidate = R13_HIGH_MAE_STAGE_ESCALATION_BRAKE_NOT_STAGE2_DELETION_V8

core residual:
- High MAE does not automatically delete Stage2 or Stage2-Actionable.
- If direct order, shipment, recognized revenue, profit conversion, tariff/cash recovery, or buyback/cancellation mechanics are present, preserve Stage2/Actionable.
- If evidence is single-variable, product-profile, tariff-only, price-pass-through-only, or post-spike contract language, cap at Stage2 or Stage4B/watch.
- Stage3-Yellow and Stage3-Green remain blocked until a second bridge appears: margin, cashflow, working-capital, repeat order, or durable capital-return execution.
- Hard 4C still requires confirmed non-price thesis break plus weak offset quality; MAE alone is insufficient.
```
