## 6. Cross-Case Accounting Trust Findings
### 6.1 Subsidy / tax credit is not automatically low quality
LG Energy Solution 2Q24 is the cleanest example. The IRA credit was larger than reported operating profit, which should block Green if treated alone, but the same evidence also contained ESS, new EV model shipments, North America volume, and operation-optimization bridges. The price path was not a hard-4C path: 180D MFE +36.62% and MAE only -4.46% from the R13 entry. Therefore, AMPC/IRA dependence should be an accounting-quality cap, not an automatic thesis break.

### 6.2 A real loss can be hard 4C only when the bridge is absent
Samsung SDI Q3 2024 had a clean hard-break profile: battery profit collapsed, discontinued-operation presentation changed comparability, and the subsequent 180D path was MFE +4.28% / MAE -51.77%. The Q1 2025 row warns against overgeneralizing: the loss was larger, but the same evidence disclosed recovery guidance, early US JV operations, 46-series ramp, and ESS/BBU opportunities; the observed path reversed sharply with 180D MFE +109.28%. The distinction is not 'loss or no loss', but whether the accounting loss is paired with credible offset/recovery bridge.

### 6.3 Inventory gains/losses and bad-debt charges need offset quality
S-Oil 2022 demonstrates why inventory gains and widened refining margins should cap Stage2-Actionable: the earnings were real but windfall-like, and 180D MAE still reached -17.63%. DL E&C 2025 demonstrates the opposite side: bad debt and cost-rate adjustment were real, yet order guidance, cost-rate improvement, and net cash support made hard 4C too aggressive; the stock-web path delivered 180D MFE +61.79% with only -5.28% MAE from entry.

### 6.4 Price validation protects against both accounting overreaction and narrative overtrust
PSK 2024 and Samsung SDI 2023 show narrative overtrust: customer capex or JV language was not enough without supplier-level orders or utilization conversion. LS ELECTRIC and Hyosung Heavy Industries show the positive control: when backlog/order/revenue/margin evidence is direct, accounting trust supports Stage2-Actionable even under a strict post-calibrated profile.

## 9. Residual Rule Candidate
```text
rule_scope = global_candidate_holdout_validation_only
canonical_archetype_rule_candidate = R13_ACCOUNTING_TRUST_OFFSET_AND_PRICE_VALIDATION_GATE
sector_specific_rule_candidate = none_R13_cross_scope
do_not_propose_new_weight_delta = true
production_scoring_changed = false
shadow_weight_only = true
```
Rule logic:
1. One-off inventory gain, subsidy credit, accounting reclassification, bad-debt recognition, or long-lead JV headline is not enough for Stage2-Actionable/Yellow unless it has a second bridge: utilization, order/backlog conversion, customer pull, margin bridge, net-cash/cost-rate offset, or recovery guidance.
2. Hard 4C requires accounting trust break **and** weak offset quality. If offset quality is explicit, route to Stage4B/watch or capped Stage2 instead of hard 4C.
3. Price validation should be used as a calibration audit, not runtime input: if accounting headline is ugly but stock-web forward path has low MAE and high MFE, the accounting headline should be reclassified as transient/offsettable in future rule review.
