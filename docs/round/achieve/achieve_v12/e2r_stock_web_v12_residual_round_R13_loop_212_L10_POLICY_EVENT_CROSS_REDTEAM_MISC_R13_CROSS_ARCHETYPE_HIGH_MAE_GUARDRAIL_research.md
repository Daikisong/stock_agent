## 14. Stage2 / Yellow / Green Comparison

The winning rows were not necessarily stronger by headline language. They were stronger by sequence. `089030` had product/customer conversion before the main expansion. `083310` and `240810` worked only in the later phase after a reset. In contrast, `039030`, early `083310`, `166090`, and early `240810` show that Stage3-Yellow/Green around a popular equipment story can be mathematically late: the observed 180D downside is much larger than the remaining upside.

## 15. 4B Local vs Full-window Timing Audit

`R13-HM-L212-04` is the cleanest 4B row. The report arrived in the peak zone; 180D MFE was only `2.93` while 180D MAE was `-58.42`. The full-4B non-price requirement remains appropriate, but R13 should tag peak-zone research/IR as `valuation_blowoff + positioning_overheat` rather than waiting for price-only collapse.

## 16. 4C Protection Audit

No representative row is a hard 4C. These are not contract-cancel, accounting-break, or qualification-failure rows. They are 4B/watch and high-MAE sizing rows. Hard 4C routing should remain untouched.

## 17. Sector-Specific Rule Candidate

For semiconductor equipment/materials rows feeding R13, evidence should be split into: direct conversion, customer/order freshness, result-only confirmation, price phase, and MAE absorption capacity. A report after a local rerating cannot be promoted the same way as a report after price reset even when the theme text is nearly identical.

## 18. Canonical-Archetype Rule Candidate

`R13_CROSS_ARCHETYPE_HIGH_MAE_GUARDRAIL` should add a phase-asymmetry ladder: `direct conversion before rerating` -> permit Stage2; `repaired price asymmetry after drawdown` -> permit Stage2/Yellow; `peak-zone report/result-only recovery` -> cap below Green or route to Stage4B/watch; `hard thesis break` -> leave to 4C only when non-price thesis evidence actually breaks.
