## 3. Thesis: C21 is an implementation gate, not a policy-word gate

For Korean financial holding companies, the tempting shortcut is to treat “저PBR / 기업가치 제고 / 밸류업” as a rerating trigger. That shortcut is leaky. C21 only becomes an E2R trigger when three gears mesh:

1. **CET1 / capital-buffer rule** — excess capital must be measurable, not rhetorical.
2. **ROE / ROTCE durability** — return target must be grounded in recurring earnings, credit-cost control and RWA discipline.
3. **Executable shareholder return** — board-approved buyback/cancellation, quantified total-shareholder-return policy, dividend predictability, or share-count reduction must exist.

When all three gears interlock, the value-up engine turns cleanly: BNK and JB are the cleanest positives in this batch, and KB/Hana show strong 180D rerating paths. When the language is broad, late, or already crowded, the same vocabulary becomes a mirror: it reflects enthusiasm but does not move the machine. Shinhan July 2024 and Woori July 2024 are the key counterexamples; KB October 2024 is a positive policy disclosure that still needed a local 4B/profit-lock overlay because the price had already rerated.

## 5. Case notes

### 5.1 KB Financial — board-approved buyback/cancellation as a clean C21 bridge

The 2024-02-07 KB trigger is a clean Stage2-Actionable case. The evidence is not merely low PBR or government value-up policy. It is board-level capital allocation: a W320bn treasury-share acquisition/cancellation route plus an existing CET1-ratio-based shareholder-return policy. The next-trading-day close entry at 67,600 produced `MFE_180D_pct=53.70` with `MAE_180D_pct=-11.69`.

The later 2024-10-24 KB sustainable value-up disclosure is a different animal. The plan is strong on paper, but the next day entry at 101,000 sat after a large rerating. The first 90 trading days produced only `MFE_90D_pct=0.89` against `MAE_90D_pct=-24.36`. That row is therefore not a fresh Green unlock; it is a local 4B/profit-lock row.

### 5.2 Hana Financial — implementation works, but staging remains necessary

Hana's 2024-10-29 official value-up disclosure and the 2025-02-04 record-profit / buyback-cancellation disclosure both show why C21 should not be dismissed as a generic policy sector. The 2024 value-up row generated `MFE_180D_pct=55.11`, and the 2025 earnings/buyback row generated `MFE_180D_pct=57.12`. But both paths also carried meaningful interim MAE, so the sector rule should add staging: Stage2-Actionable is correct, instant Green is not.

### 5.3 Shinhan and Woori — good words, bad timing or incomplete execution

Shinhan's July 2024 value-up plan had real ROE/ROTCE/CET1 targets, but the price path shows why C21 needs a local-4B overlay. Entry at 60,700 produced only `MFE_180D_pct=6.43` and `MAE_180D_pct=-29.98`. This is not evidence failure; it is timing/crowding failure.

Woori's July 2024 value-up target row is weaker. It generated only `MFE_180D_pct=7.16` and carried `MAE_180D_pct=-15.86`. The residual says policy ambition alone should remain Stage2-watch unless CET1 buffer and actual buyback/cancellation execution are visible.

### 5.4 BNK and JB — regional bank positives when the return bridge is specific

BNK and JB are the most useful source-repair rows because they show that regional banks can be positive C21 rows when the return bridge is quantified. BNK's 2024-10-30 value-up route produced `MFE_180D_pct=70.02` with only `MAE_180D_pct=-2.01`. JB's 2024-09-24 voluntary disclosure produced `MFE_180D_pct=48.37` with `MAE_180D_pct=-1.40`. These rows support a canonical rule that rewards executable capital-return design, not simply market-cap size.

## 6. Current-profile residual

The global v12 calibrated profile already blocks pure price-only blowoffs and requires non-price evidence for full 4B. C21 still leaves four residual errors:

1. **Low-PBR policy proxy leakage** — policy/sector vocabulary can be mistaken for firm-level executable return.
2. **Late value-up disclosure overcredit** — good capital-return disclosure after a fast rerating can still produce high MAE.
3. **Regional-bank undercredit** — high ROE plus quantified return targets in smaller financial holdings can work better than generic large-bank value-up headlines.
4. **Staging gap** — even successful C21 rows often carry double-digit MAE before the 180D rerating path appears.

Rule text:

```text
For C21, grant Stage2-Actionable credit only when at least two of the following are visible as-of trigger_date:
1. quantified CET1 / capital-buffer threshold or excess-capital policy;
2. ROE / ROTCE target supported by recurring earnings or credit-cost/RWA discipline;
3. board-approved buyback, cancellation, dividend, share-count reduction or total-shareholder-return execution.

If the same evidence appears after a fast low-PBR/value-up rerating, cap the row at Stage4B/local profit-lock unless the 30D/90D forward path remains low-MAE and the new capital-return execution is materially incremental.

If the evidence is only government value-up policy, low-PBR screen inclusion, or sector beta, keep it Stage2-watch or narrative-only.
```
