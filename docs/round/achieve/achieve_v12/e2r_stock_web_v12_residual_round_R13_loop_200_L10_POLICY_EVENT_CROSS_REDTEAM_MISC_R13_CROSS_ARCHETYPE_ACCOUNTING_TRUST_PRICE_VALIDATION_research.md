## 6. Cross-Case Findings

### 6.1 Inventory and accounting uncertainty should become 4B/watch before 4C

Shinsegae International and F&F illustrate the same accounting-trust boundary from different sides. Inventory NRV, allowance, clearance, domestic/duty-free weakness, and margin pressure are real risk evidence, but the forward windows still show recoverable price paths. R13 should keep this as a local 4B/watch guard unless issuer-level repeated margin failure, write-down cascade, liquidity stress, or weak offset quality is confirmed.

### 6.2 Direct second bridge protects Actionable, but high MAE blocks Green

FILA/Misto, Youngone, Gamsung, Silicon2, Cosmax, Kolmar, APR, and VT have recognizable second bridges: inventory asset decline, operating-profit conversion, overseas subsidiary or ODM export growth, and global channel revenue. These rows should not be deleted merely because several later printed high MAE. The correct residual use is a Yellow/Green escalation brake.

### 6.3 Product-profile and forecast-only rows stay capped

The Nature Holdings and Hanssem show why Stage2 should remain capped when the evidence is forecast-only, cost-cutting-only, or demand recovery is not yet visible. Strong category narrative without inventory/margin/cash conversion is not Actionable.

### 6.4 China loss / one-product virality are not automatic 4C

Amorepacific and LG H&H show regional weakness with offset routes. APR and VT show high-growth global product concentration with high MAE. These are not hard 4C by themselves. They should be watched until regional operating-profit recovery, repeat reorder, or channel durability confirms or breaks the thesis.
