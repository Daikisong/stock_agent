## 1. Objective

This R13 loop is not a new sector-positive hunt. It compresses the recent L8 source-sector rows from C26, C27, and C28 into a cross-archetype high-MAE holdout. The target error is the same one that keeps appearing across platform, content, and software/security cases: **a real second bridge can still draw down hard before the 180D winner path shows up, while a single headline can produce a price path that looks good enough to tempt Stage3 but lacks repeatability.**

The rule under test is therefore narrow: high MAE should behave as a stage-escalation brake, not as a universal deletion rule. Stage2 and Stage2-Actionable may survive when direct ad/commerce margin, contract-retention, launch/revenue, or recurring-service bridges exist. Yellow/Green should remain blocked until repeat revenue, retention, cashflow, or margin durability appears in more than one evidence family.

## 6. Cross-case diagnosis

### 6.1 Stage2 should not be deleted by high MAE alone

NAVER, SOOP, Cafe24, Devsisters, CJ ENM, Douzone, and SM show that a row can be painful within 90D/180D while still carrying a real bridge. In these cases the mistake is not necessarily the Stage2/Actionable label. The mistake would be letting that label climb to Yellow or Green before the bridge repeats through margin, retention, paid traffic, launch revenue, or cashflow.

### 6.2 Single headline remains capped even when 180D MFE later looks attractive

Kakao, Hancom, Nasmedia, Pearl Abyss, and some content/platform rows show why product profile, AI/platform language, pipeline visibility, or one-quarter rebound should not be treated as durable rerating evidence. If issuer-level recurring revenue, contract renewal, operating leverage, launch monetization, or retention is missing, the row remains capped Stage2 or Stage4B/watch.

### 6.3 Positive controls remain useful but do not loosen Green

GENIANS and Krafton are useful controls. Their forward paths were cleaner than most holdouts, and both had more concrete bridge quality than a pure theme row. Even so, R13 should not use the price path itself to loosen Green. Green still requires repeated evidence-family confirmation around cashflow, retention, recurring revenue, or durable monetization.

Rule text:

> In L8-derived platform/content/software/security rows, 180D high MAE should first brake Stage3-Yellow and Stage3-Green escalation. It should not erase Stage2 or Stage2-Actionable when a direct second bridge exists. Single-headline rows without repeat revenue, retention, launch monetization, ad/commerce margin, or contract-renewal evidence remain capped even if their later MFE is high.

Operational implications:

```text
- direct second bridge present + high MAE -> keep Stage2/Actionable, block Yellow/Green
- single headline + high MFE -> do not loosen Green, cap Stage2 or watch
- ugly quarter with offset -> Stage4B/watch before hard 4C
- hard 4C -> requires non-price thesis break + weak offset quality
- R13 file -> no immediate production weight delta
```
