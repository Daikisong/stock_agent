## 1. Selection rationale

C09 remains a static Priority 0 bucket in the No-Repeat ledger. Earlier C09 loops already covered first-order HBM tool leaders, AFM/metrology, wafer testers, ALD/SiC process components, and material-proxy guards. This follow-up tests the boundary where the model can still fail: fab-subsystem tools, process-critical HBM components, direct HBM cleaning PO after price crowding, and HBM-adjacent PCB/substrate/material proxies.

This is not a claim that every semiconductor-adjacent stock belongs in C09. The purpose is the opposite: compress the fine/deep labels into a stricter canonical rule that credits real equipment/process bottlenecks and blocks substrate/material/product-label proxies unless the order/revenue/margin conversion is fresh versus price.

## 4. Evidence notes

### 4.1 Unisem / 036200

Unisem is a scrubber/chiller fab-subsystem supplier. The Q1 2024 earnings note links chiller sales growth to semiconductor capex recovery and HBM-related investment. This should not be ignored by a C09 runner merely because the product is a subsystem rather than a TC bonder or wafer tester. Still, the stock-web path opens a -38.75% 90D MAE after only 25.93% 90D MFE, so the right label is Stage2-Actionable with local 4B overlay, not durable Yellow.

### 4.2 Mico / 059090

Mico is a process-critical component row: the 2024-05-08 source describes TC-bonder pulse heater localization, customer quality evaluation, and HBM manufacturing-equipment relevance. That deserves early C09 credit, but the row is still pre-approval/pre-revenue. The price path confirms a fast MFE followed by a deep drawdown, so a profit-lock guard is essential.

### 4.3 Zeus / 079370

Zeus is the cleanest direct-equipment evidence but a bad entry-context row. By 2024-05-20, HBM cleaning equipment PO evidence existed, yet the story had already been crowded from January and a share-count reset had occurred before the selected May entry. The usable May row produced only 5.44% 90D MFE and -44.50% 90D MAE. C09 therefore needs a freshness-vs-price gate even for real HBM equipment.

### 4.4 FST / 036810

FST has real semiconductor adjacency through pellicle/chiller routes. The residual error is treating product identity as order conversion. Without a named customer order, qualification milestone, or margin bridge, the row should stay Stage4B or Stage2-watch after a price extension.

### 4.5 Wonik Materials / 104830

Wonik Materials is a successful price-path row, but it is a wrong-canonical warning. Precursor and special-gas exposure can work as a semiconductor recovery/material route, yet it should not be used to validate C09 equipment scoring unless the process-component bottleneck is explicit.

### 4.6 Simtech / 222800, Daeduck Electronics / 353200, TLB / 356860

These rows are HBM/DDR5-adjacent substrate and PCB proxies. They help teach the model where not to overextend C09. The May 2024 Simtech/Daeduck article explicitly says both were not yet HBM FC-BGA suppliers; TLB had DDR5 module PCB exposure but not advanced equipment. All three rows show limited MFE and large MAE, supporting proxy blocking.
