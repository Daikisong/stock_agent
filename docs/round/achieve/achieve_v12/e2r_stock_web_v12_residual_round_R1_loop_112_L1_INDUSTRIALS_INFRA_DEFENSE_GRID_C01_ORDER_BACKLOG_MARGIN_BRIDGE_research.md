## 4. Residual interpretation

C01 behaves like the market’s order book ledger. The entry looks attractive when the book is thick, but the profit only becomes real when the backlog moves across the factory floor, into delivery, then into gross margin, working capital release, and FCF. In this pass, high-MFE defense and grid rows show why the bridge must preserve genuine positives, while construction and EPC rows show why a nominal order/backlog label can be a trap when PF exposure, cost overrun, or working-capital pressure is the dominant mechanism.

The recurring failure mode is not “backlog is useless.” It is that generic backlog language becomes an umbrella too large for the rain it is supposed to stop. C02, C03, C05, and C30 each have their own physics. C01 should remain the shared trunk only when delivery and margin conversion are visible; otherwise the case should be rerouted to the more specific branch.
