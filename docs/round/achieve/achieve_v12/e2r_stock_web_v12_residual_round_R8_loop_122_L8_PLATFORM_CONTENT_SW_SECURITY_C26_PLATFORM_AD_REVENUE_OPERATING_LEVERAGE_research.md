## 4. Case Notes
### 4.1 067160 SOOP — forced user inventory migration worked, but Green confirmation was late
Twitch Korea exit is not ordinary ad-market vocabulary. It is a forced migration shock in livestreaming user/streamer inventory. SOOP was one of the few direct domestic receiving platforms. The conservative next-trading-day entry after the 2023-12-06 evidence date produced `MFE_90D=82.25%`, `MAE_90D=-6.01%`, and `MFE_180D=87.73%`.

The later Q1 proof row is economically cleaner but late: Q1 showed strong platform/ad/OPM confirmation, yet entry at 2024-04-30 captured only `MFE_180D=27.48%` with `MAE_180D=-29.34%`. Green lateness ratio versus the early Stage2 entry and the 180D peak is `0.5387`. The price-only local extension row at 2024-02-20 has local 4B proximity `0.6651` and full-window proximity `0.6235`, but absent non-price thesis break it should remain 4B-watch rather than full 4B.

### 4.2 035720 Kakao — headline platform growth is not enough
Kakao's Q1 2024 headline numbers contained strong operating profit growth and TalkBiz/platform growth, but the stock path after a conservative next-day entry was `MFE_90D=2.62%` and `MAE_90D=-31.17%`. This is the exact C26 false-positive shape: mega-platform growth vocabulary without a clean consensus beat, margin rerating, or single-business operating-leverage bridge.

### 4.3 089600 Nasmedia — OTT ad growth offset by consolidated drag
Nasmedia had isolated OTT/digital-ad growth language, but consolidated Q1 revenue was roughly flat/down and OP declined. Its 180D path (`MFE_180D=4.18%`, `MAE_180D=-26.60%`) shows why C26 must not reward subsegment growth without consolidated operating leverage.

### 4.4 216050 Incross — operating leverage exists, but early MAE guard matters
Incross Q3 2024 is the constructive counterweight to Kakao/Nasmedia. Revenue, OP, OPM, media-rep handling amount, and advertiser recovery all line up. The stock still had weak early follow-through (`MFE_90D=2.18%`, `MAE_90D=-14.60%`) before a delayed 180D move (`MFE_180D=20.49%`). This supports Stage2-Yellow or Stage2-Actionable, but not immediate Stage3-Green without a second confirmation or one-off-cost cleanup.
## 5. Proposed C26 Shadow Rule Candidate
```text
rule_id = C26_AD_REVENUE_OPERATING_LEVERAGE_GATE_V1
positive_gate = exogenous user/ad inventory migration shock OR confirmed handling_amount/revenue bridge
confirmation_gate = OPM expansion, revision surprise/consensus beat, or second independent evidence family
block_gate = isolated platform/TalkBiz/OTT growth if consolidated OP misses, platform-biz drag exists, or multi-segment complexity absorbs the signal
stage_behavior = early Stage2 allowed for forced migration; Green requires monetization/OPM proof; price-only extension is 4B-watch not full 4B
```
Mechanically, C26 should act like a toll gate, not a megaphone. It should not amplify every word that sounds like `advertising recovery`; it should let through only the traffic that actually pays a toll: user/advertiser inventory turning into revenue, revenue turning into margin, and margin turning into revision or durable cash-flow visibility.
