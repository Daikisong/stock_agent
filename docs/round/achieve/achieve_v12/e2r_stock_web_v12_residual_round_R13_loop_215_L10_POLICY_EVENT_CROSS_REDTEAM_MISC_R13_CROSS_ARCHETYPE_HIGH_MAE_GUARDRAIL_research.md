## 5. Cross-archetype findings

The L2 HBM/test/equipment chain behaves like a gear train connected by a belt. A named customer order, sold-out capacity, qualification path, or recognized equipment revenue is a real tooth on the gear. But the market often stretches the belt long before repeated revenue, margin, cashflow, or qualification-pass evidence arrives. That is why the same trigger can produce large MFE and still inflict a deep MAE.

### 5.1 Preserve early Stage2 when the second bridge is real
SK hynix, ISC, LEENO, Okins, FST, and Wonik IPS show that issuer-level second bridges should not be erased merely because the forward path has a large drawdown. The bridge is a valid smoke signal; the drawdown only tells us not to let the signal become Green too easily.

### 5.2 Use high MAE as an escalation brake
Most rows in this holdout have 180D MAE at or below -20%, and several fall below -40%. That should block Stage3-Yellow / Stage3-Green unless repeated conversion evidence appears. It should not automatically convert every row to hard 4C.

### 5.3 Profile-only and late-extension rows stay capped
Substrate sympathy, FCBGA/MLB product profile, test-socket roadmap, HPA/HPO or EUV-pellicle technology language, and post-rerating equipment expectation are not enough for Actionable/Yellow without issuer-level order, shipment, customer qualification, recognized revenue, or margin conversion.
