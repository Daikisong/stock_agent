## 5. Current Calibrated Profile Stress Test

The current calibrated profile already blocks price-only blowoff and raises the bridge requirement, but C15 still needs a narrower commodity-spread distinction:

1. **Direct project/export spread winners** — SeAH Steel and Nexteel have company-level pipe/export margin routes. Stage2-Actionable is valid, but Green should still wait for spread duration or cash/FCF conversion.
2. **Actual-result but late-cycle windfalls** — Daehan Steel 2022 and Husteel 2023 show record-profit evidence after spread had already expanded. Stage2-Actionable may remain usable, but should be capped because the price path quickly peaks and then draws down.
3. **Demand/profit collapse rows** — Korea Steel 2024 has operating-profit and volume collapse evidence. This is not merely local 4B; hard 4C is justified because the issuer-level spread thesis broke.
4. **Slowdown without collapse** — Daehan Steel 2023 demand/price/volume weakness should stay as Stage4B/watch, not hard 4C, because the 180D price path did not confirm a thesis-break collapse.

## 6. Proposed Shadow Rule Candidate

```text

sector_rule_candidate = L4_MATERIAL_SPREAD_PERSISTENCE_AND_ISSUER_BREAK_GATE
```

Rule text:

- Spread / commodity price / record profit headline alone cannot create Stage3-Yellow or Stage3-Green.
- Stage2-Actionable needs at least one direct second bridge: issuer-level margin bridge, project volume recognition, export ASP/ton-spread, order/shipment route, or reported operating-profit conversion.
- Inventory gain, high-base comparison, or record-profit evidence that appears after a fast spread run gets an Actionable cap and Green blocker.
- Hard 4C requires issuer-level volume/profit collapse, spread-thesis break, or margin bridge failure; price drawdown alone is not enough.
- Slowdown evidence without issuer-level thesis break remains Stage4B/watch.
